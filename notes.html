Your Cheat Sheet

For your eyes only — read through before the conversation

How to Bring It Up

"Hey, so I came across this company called AutoMinds that actually went ahead and redesigned our website. I was really impressed with what they did and I wanted to show you. I think it could be a big upgrade for us."

If He Asks "What's Different?"

Keep it high-level. Don't list specifics — let the site speak for itself. Just say:

  • It's a completely custom design — not a WordPress template anymore
  • It actually looks like a real cybersecurity & defense contractor site now
  • It highlights your leadership, your TEDCO appointment, your credentials
  • All the services are broken down properly — Systems Engineering, Cybersecurity, Ops Management
  • It works great on phones too, not just desktop
  • They even added our NAICS codes, contract vehicles, and certifications

How to Show It

  • Pull up the scan page on your phone
  • Say "Here, scan this — you can see the whole thing right now"
  • Let him look through it on his own phone
  • While he's scrolling, mention how professional it looks compared to what we have now

If He Asks About Price

Don't bring up pricing first — let him ask. If he does:

"They said the website is $500 one-time, and then $50 a month for hosting, maintenance, updates, and security. And if we ever need anything else — like automation tools or dashboards — they can do that too. We'd just set up a consultation."

If He Seems Interested

"I can send you the link so you can look at it more later. They left their contact info on there too — it's a company called AutoMinds. Or I can connect you with them directly."

If He's Not Sure Yet

"No pressure at all — the site is already built and live so it's not going anywhere. You can take a look whenever. I just thought it was worth showing you because it's a big step up from what we have."

Remember

  • You're an employee who found something great — not a salesperson
  • Let the website do the selling. Just show it.
  • Don't list what's wrong with the current site — let him see the difference
  • If pricing comes up, the card has "Interested? View Pricing" — let him tap it
  • Stay casual and confident — you're helping the company, not pushing a product
